My Most Impactful Projects
Pre-term infant diet calculator

Diets (i.e. caloric & micronutrient intake) are everything in the NICU! Pre-term infant diets are calculated based on the baby's weight and anemic status.

Before my project:

These calculations were done manually which made them time-consuming and expensive; it was one dietitian's full-time job to solely calculate all our NICU babies' diets by hand.

The calculations were often wrong which led to suboptimal nutrition for our pre-term infants!

The diets were a pain to summarize in a note so they were rarely charted, leaving the rest of the care team in the dark.

Results of my project:

My calculator saved the unit 40 hours of work a week (or $50,000 annually).

Our pre-term infants were getting the proper nutrition they needed which led to better clinical outcomes!

The macro button that screenshotted the results allowed the results to be easily pasted into the patient's chart where the whole care team can see.

Milk lab relational database

Breast milk (AKA "liquid gold") is vital for NICU babies because it contains white blood cells & antibodies made by mom for the baby to fight against bacteria & viruses.

Each bottle must be labeled with the baby's info and the date & time the milk was pumped before it's stored in the fridge/freezer. This ensures that the bottle goes to the right baby and the milk is not expired.

Before my project:

There was no centralized system to inventory the milk. IT tried to help but gave us incomplete solutions. Bottles were being recorded in 2 different paper logs and haphazardly in the EHR.

Checking for expired milk was time-consuming & tedious so it rarely got done by the already overburdened staff.

As a consequence, lots of breast milk had expired without our knowledge.

Results of my project:

I provided a centralized & effective system. Each baby got their own profile, the bottles were sorted by expiry date, and we were able to track the location of bottles (fridge or freezer) & mom's milk supply.

Our nurses had more time for patient care which led to better clinical outcomes.

NO MORE WASTED BREAST MILK FOR OUR CRITICALLY-ILL AND PRE-TERM BABIES!!!

Dashboards

Dashboards are a great, intuitive way to display data. Over the years, I have built many dashboards for the NICU, L&D, MICC, and ambulatory surgery center. This allowed them to easily track key metrics and gain business insight!

Before my project:

Reports were manually generated, making them error-prone, time-consuming, and expensive; on average, it took admin 1 day to calculate weekly reports every week and 1 week to calculate quarterly reports every quarter.

Depending on the audience (e.g. executives, staff, patients), different reports on the same data had to be manually generated.

Quarterly reports were done last minute which meant we were often late to discover bad trends in key metrics!

All our data was stored locally on the units' shared drive, which meant staff had to risk going into the hospital and getting COVID to access the data.

Promotions were based off of data that only administration had access to, which caused tension amongst teams (e.g. "Why did Maria get a promotion and I didn't?!").

Results of my project:

My dashboards saved the unit 376 hours of work per quarter (or $50,000 annually.)

The data can be easily filtered to suit the respective audience's needs.

Metrics are displayed in real-time so bad trends can be spotted early and dealt with accordingly!

My dashboards are synchronized with hospital intranet permissions and allowed remote access.

My dashboards creates work incentives, as well as promotes team unity by providing ownership and visibility of work.
